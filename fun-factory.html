Math Problem of the Week

Can you crack it?

Week of Sep 22–Sep 28, 2025

A rectangle has a perimeter of 54 cm. Its length is 3 cm more than twice its width. What are the rectangle's dimensions?

Hint

Let width be w. Then length is 2w + 3. Use 2(l + w) = 54.

Show Solution

2[(2w + 3) + w] = 54 ⇒ 2(3w + 3) = 54 ⇒ 6w + 6 = 54 ⇒ 6w = 48 ⇒ w = 8. Then l = 2·8 + 3 = 19. Dimensions: 8 cm × 19 cm.
